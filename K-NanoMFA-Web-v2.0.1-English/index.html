Static model structure

Effective regional annual input→Product allocation→Use-stage release+Same-year EoL→Waste treatment→Regional destinations and PECs

All mass not released during use reaches the end-of-life system in the selected year. Regional scaling is applied before product allocation.

Dynamic model structure

Effective regional annual inputs→Product cohorts→In-use stock→Time-distributed use release and Weibull EoL→Treatment↻Reuse/recycling/landfill stocks

Each entered annual trajectory is interpreted using the selected market-input geography. Dynamic soil and sediment PECs use accumulated screening stocks with first-order residence-time loss.

Dynamic horizon and market-input trajectory

Direct-input mode uses the aggregate trajectory. Product-informed mode derives annual product-specific ENM inputs from market, content, and nano-enabled trends.

Time trends, uncertainty, and evidence

Annual changes are relative percentages. Product-specific coefficients of variation are propagated in probabilistic runs as independent lognormal input terms.

Product-informed calculations track nanomaterial mass, not total product mass. Product-market data remain screening inputs unless supported by documented inventories. Product-specific trajectories are used in dynamic inflow-driven runs; stock-driven inverse runs retain their aggregate solver and use the active product allocation.

Product-category settings

Category names and use-stage releases remain editable. In direct-input mode, allocation shares are user inputs; in product-informed mode, they are calculated from product-specific ENM inputs.

Dynamic lifetime and release timing

Mean lifetime and Weibull shape determine delayed EoL. The assigned use-stage release is spread over the specified duration.

Environmental-media PEC settings

Domain defaults are generated from wastewater flow, land area, and effective national freshwater flow, then remain fully editable.

Air and surface-water PECs are annual flow-through screening concentrations. Static soil and sediment PECs use a steady-state residence-time approximation. Dynamic soil and sediment PECs accumulate annual screening stocks with first-order loss. This module is not a full multimedia fate model.

Multiple nanomaterials and co-exposure

Run material-specific MFA models in parallel within one geographic domain. Concentrations remain material resolved. A summed mass concentration is shown only when the selected tracking bases are compatible; it never implies toxicological equivalence.

Material components

Each component retains its own product categories, use releases, treatment coefficients, lifetime distribution, and nanoform assumptions. “Capture editor” replaces a component snapshot with the current single-material editor.

All component inputs use the currently selected country/domain and market-input geography. In dynamic mode, component-specific growth and active years create separate trajectories.

Co-formulated product or blend groups

A formulation group defines a total nanomaterial-blend input, shared use-release pattern, shared end-of-life routing, and shared product lifetime. Material fractions must total 100%; material-specific treatment coefficients remain independent.

Component-based mixture screening

Enter only comparable PNECs. The app calculates component HQ = PEC/PNEC and sums HQs within a user-defined assessment group when the medium and endpoint descriptions are compatible. A blank or zero PNEC excludes that component-medium pair.

Optional interaction-adjusted screeningβ = 0 by default

Interaction coefficients are not predicted by the app. Enter β only for the specified material pair, medium, endpoint, concentration range, and nanoform when directly relevant evidence is available. Adjusted HI = HI + ΣβHQᵢHQⱼ.

Data-quality matrix and group-specific uncertainty

Scores range from 1 (poor) to 5 (strong). The coefficient of variation is applied to the corresponding group in probabilistic simulation.

Nanoform-resolved environmental releases

Allocate air, water, and soil releases among free/aggregated nanoform, matrix-associated material, transformed or dissolved form, and unknown form. These are editable screening assumptions.

Model fitness flags

Flags are diagnostic. They do not convert a screening model into a validated national inventory or a mechanistic environmental fate model.

Nanoform state tracking

Optional v1.5 post-processing separates the conserved MFA mass into product-associated, particulate, dissolved, transformed, unresolved, and non-nano states. Disabling this option preserves the v1.4.1 output pathway.

Initial product-state allocation

The six editable fractions must sum to 100%. Non-nano or destroyed mass is generated only by treatment or transformation sinks.

Environmental-state mapping

The existing four release-form categories are subdivided into free versus aggregated and dissolved versus transformed particulate forms.

Review derived destination-state profiles

These mass-normalized profiles are derived from the selected material and embedding presets. They do not replace process-specific experimental evidence.

Mass-conserving screening sensitivity analysis

Each parameter group is perturbed by the selected percentage. Compositional vectors are renormalised so the mass balance remains closed.

Dynamic nanoform-resolved environmental fate

The optional v2.0.1 engine propagates matrix-bound, free-particle, small-aggregate, large-aggregate, dissolved, transformed, and unresolved forms through air, water, soil, and active sediment. The default exact linear solver integrates the full coupled state-transition system without timestep-dependent cascade delay; the legacy substep solver remains available for comparison.

Editable state-transition and intermedia rate constants

Rates are first-order constants in y⁻¹. The exact solver integrates all active transfers simultaneously. The legacy solver is retained only for reproducibility and refinement auditing. External losses are reported separately from active stocks.

Correlated kinetic-parameter uncertainty

Rates are grouped as dissolution, aggregation/settling, transport/remobilization, and transformation. A Gaussian-copula lognormal multiplier applies the entered common between-group correlation.

International trade and apparent-consumption accounting

When enabled, the entered market input is interpreted as domestic production. Apparent consumption is calculated as production + imports − exports − technosphere inventory increase before geographic allocation.

Annual trade accounting

Dynamic rows are generated from the current model horizon. Values are national totals before regional allocation.

Bilateral partner matrix

Partner shares allocate total imports and exports for transparent reporting. Import and export shares should each sum to 100%.

Inflow-driven or stock-driven dynamic MFA

Stock-driven mode infers the primary market input required to approach an entered end-of-year in-use stock trajectory. If inherited stock already exceeds the target, primary input is constrained to zero and the target gap is reported.

Constrained data reconciliation

Observed and modelled flows are combined using uncertainty weights and adjusted under one exact mass-balance equation. Non-negativity is enforced by an active-set projection.

Bayesian calibration against an observed result

A scalar multiplier is calibrated by grid-based Bayesian updating. The likelihood and prior are lognormal, making the result appropriate for positive parameters and concentration observations.

Reduced-form mechanistic multimedia fate

Air, water, soil, and active sediment are linked by first-order intermedia transfer, advective loss, burial, resuspension, and transformation. The module preserves mass at each numerical substep but is not a replacement for a calibrated SimpleBox4nano implementation.

Independent validation dataset

Enter measured flow, stock, or concentration values. The app retrieves the matching model output and reports residuals, RMSE, bias, MAPE, uncertainty coverage, and reduced chi-square.

Screening life-cycle burden indicator

Editable climate factors estimate direct handling burdens and an avoided-primary-production credit. This is a co-indicator, not an ISO-compliant life-cycle assessment.

Country and subnational-domain coupling

The selected country is first resolved to a national domain. Korea additionally provides 17 bundled subnational domains, while user-defined countries may contain one national and multiple custom subnational domains. A national input may be allocated to a subnational domain by population, sewered population, or wastewater-flow share, or the entered value may be treated as a direct domain input. Country-domain wastewater flow, sludge production, effective freshwater flow, land area, and atmospheric volume control the PEC denominators. An optional country-waste coupling replaces material-specific EoL shares with an editable country screening preset.

Static model

The static model allocates the effective regional annual input to product categories, calculates use-stage releases, and sends all remaining mass to end-of-life treatment in the same year.

Static probabilistic model

The temporal boundary remains one year. Uncertain allocation, release, EoL, and treatment parameters are repeatedly sampled while preserving percentage-vector closure.

Dynamic probabilistic model

The dynamic model is inflow-driven and cohort-based. Annual effective regional inputs enter product-specific stocks. Residual product mass leaves stock according to a Weibull lifetime distribution. Recovered material may return as secondary input after a delay, reuse stock returns to EoL after a delay, and landfill releases are calculated from accumulated landfill stock.

v2.0.1 numerical-stability and interpretation update

  • The v1.5 state-resolved release inventory can be propagated through air, surface water, soil, and active sediment as matrix-bound, free-particle, small-aggregate, large-aggregate, dissolved, transformed-particulate, and unresolved forms.
  • The default exact coupled linear solver removes substep-dependent cascade delay; the previous substep solver remains available with an optional doubled-substep convergence audit. External losses are reported separately as advection, burial, irreversible retention, or transfer outside the selected tracking basis.
  • Static MFA emissions can be iterated to a screening steady state, whereas dynamic MFA emissions produce annual form- and compartment-resolved trajectories.
  • Storm-event remobilization, external annual-emission CSV coupling, and correlated kinetic uncertainty are optional.
  • Disabling the v2.0.1 engine preserves the v1.5 calculation pathway.

  • Product-specific market growth, ENM-content change, and nano-enabled-share change generate annual dynamic input trajectories.
  • Product-specific input uncertainty and evidence metadata are stored in scenario and audit exports.

Interpretation boundary

  • Bundled product shares, release factors, lifetimes, and transfer coefficients are screening priors, not measured national inventories.
  • Population- or flow-based geographic allocation is a transparent downscaling assumption, not a measured country or regional nanomaterial market inventory.
  • The base soil and sediment PECs remain screening stock calculations. The optional v2.0.1 engine resolves operational aggregation, dissolution, settling, resuspension, and transformation with coupled first-order kinetics, but does not reproduce site-specific geochemistry or hydrodynamics.
  • “Transformation / loss” leaves the selected nanoform tracking basis.

Advanced capabilities in v1.2

  • Trade-balanced apparent consumption with annual totals and bilateral partner allocation.
  • Stock-driven inverse cohort MFA with annual target-stock diagnostics.
  • Uncertainty-weighted constrained reconciliation and grid-based Bayesian scalar calibration.
  • A mass-conserving four-compartment multimedia fate model with intermedia transfer and external sinks.
  • User-supplied validation datasets and a screening climate-burden co-indicator.

Remaining scientific qualification

  • Both fate modules remain spatially aggregated and first-order. The v2.0.1 engine distinguishes free particles and two aggregate classes, but it does not solve continuous particle-size distributions, natural-colloid heteroaggregation kernels, pH-dependent dissolution chemistry, watershed hydraulics, or spatial river networks.
  • Trade accounting represents mass of the tracked nanoform and does not include a full multi-regional input–output supply chain.
  • The life-cycle burden module is an editable screening co-indicator, not a complete ISO 14040/14044 assessment.
  • Validation and calibration quality remain limited by the availability and comparability of measured nanoform data.
